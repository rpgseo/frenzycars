faq:
  - q: 'Can a student get a car loan with no credit history?'
    a: 'Yes, but you will almost certainly need a cosigner or a larger down payment. Lenders see no credit as high risk, so solo approval is rare and usually expensive. A creditworthy cosigner can unlock APRs under 8%, while no-credit solo loans often run 15% or higher.'
  - q: 'What credit score do you need for a student car loan?'
    a: 'Most lenders want at least a 660 for a standard auto loan. Subprime lenders go down to around 500, but rates climb steeply below 620. A cosigner with a 700+ score dramatically improves both approval odds and the rate you are offered.'
  - q: 'Is it better to finance through a bank, credit union, or dealership?'
    a: 'Credit unions typically offer the lowest rates — often 1 to 3 points below banks — and are more flexible with thin credit files. Always get pre-approved before visiting a dealer; dealership financing can be convenient but frequently carries a markup on the rate.'
  - q: 'How much down payment should a student put on a car?'
    a: 'Aim for at least 10% of the purchase price, ideally 20%. A larger down payment reduces the loan amount, lowers your monthly payment, and helps you avoid being underwater — owing more than the car is worth — the moment you drive off the lot.'
  - q: 'Can an international student get a car loan in the US?'
    a: 'It is difficult but possible. You will usually need an SSN or ITIN, proof of income, a US-based cosigner, and sometimes a larger down payment. Some lenders specialize in loans for students without a US credit history, though rates are typically higher than standard loans.'
