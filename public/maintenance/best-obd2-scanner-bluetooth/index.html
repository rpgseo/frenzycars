What is the best Bluetooth OBD2 scanner?

Est. cost: $15–$80 USD

Quick answer

The OBDLink LX is the best overall Bluetooth OBD2 scanner thanks to fast data refresh, wide app compatibility, and rock-solid reliability. For most drivers, the vgate iCar Pro offers the best value, with low power draw and a sleep mode that protects your battery. Just remember Android phones use Bluetooth Classic dongles, while iPhones require a BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) adapter.

A Bluetooth OBD2 scanner is a small plug-in dongle that reads the diagnostic port under the dash and beams the data wirelessly to your phone. It turns any smartphone into a live diagnostic tool, letting you read and clear check engine codes, view real-time sensor data, and monitor fuel economy or performance. The key distinction is that Bluetooth Classic dongles only work with Android, while BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) adapters are required to pair with an iPhone. Pick the right type for your phone, download a compatible app, and you have pro-grade diagnostics in your pocket.

How we chose

  • Protocol and vehicle compatibility, including full CAN bus support and coverage of 2008-and-newer vehicles.
  • App support across popular platforms like Torque, Car Scanner, and BimmerCode.
  • Data refresh speed, because faster polling means smoother live gauges and more accurate sensor readings.
  • Build quality and power management, including auto-sleep features that prevent battery drain.
  • Price, balancing cheap ELM327 clones against reliable brands that justify their premium.

Buying tips

  • Match the dongle to your phone: most cheap adapters use Bluetooth Classic and only work with Android, while iPhone owners must choose a BLE model.
  • Unplug the scanner when the engine is off, as some budget dongles draw power constantly and can drain a weak battery over several days.
  • Pair the adapter with a free app like Car Scanner first to confirm it works with your vehicle before paying for a premium app.
  • Check that your vehicle is 2008 or newer for guaranteed CAN bus compliance.

Frequently asked questions

Do Bluetooth OBD2 scanners work on iPhone?

Only BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) adapters work with an iPhone, because iOS does not support the Bluetooth Classic protocol that most cheap dongles use. Look for a dongle explicitly labeled BLE or Bluetooth 4.0+ to ensure it pairs with your iPhone.

Is a cheap ELM327 OBD2 scanner safe?

Genuine branded ELM327-based dongles are generally safe, but ultra-cheap counterfeit clones can have poor voltage regulation and may interfere with your car's CAN bus. For peace of mind, spend a little more on a reputable brand like OBDLink, vgate, or BAFX.

What is the best app for an OBD2 scanner?

Torque Pro and Car Scanner are the most popular for Android thanks to customizable live gauges and deep code definitions, while BimmerCode is ideal for BMW owners wanting coding features. iPhone users get great results with Car Scanner and OBD JScan.

Can an OBD2 scanner clear a check engine light?

Yes, once you have read the diagnostic code and actually fixed the underlying problem, most apps let you clear the code and reset the check engine light. If the light returns immediately, the fault has not been resolved and the code will set again.

Does an OBD2 scanner drain the car battery?

Premium dongles like the OBDLink LX and vgate iCar Pro have a sleep mode that cuts power when the engine is off, so they are safe to leave plugged in. Cheap clones with no power management can slowly drain your battery over several days, so it is best to unplug them when parked.
