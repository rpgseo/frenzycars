Est. cost: $60–$200 USD

Quick answer

The NOCO Boost Plus GB70 is the best overall portable jump starter thanks to its 2000-amp peak output, spark-proof clamps, and reverse-polarity protection that suits most gas and diesel vehicles. The right choice depends on matching peak amps to your engine size: a 1000-amp unit handles 4- and 6-cylinder gas engines, while 2000 amps or more is recommended for V8s and diesel trucks. Safety features like reverse-polarity and spark-proof technology are essential to prevent damage to your battery and electronics.

A lithium jump starter is a smarter, safer upgrade over jumper cables because it does not require a second vehicle, fits in a glovebox, and doubles as a power bank or flashlight in emergencies. The key spec is peak amps, which should be matched to the largest engine you own: smaller gas engines need roughly 1000 amps, while diesel trucks and big V8s demand 2000 amps or more. Unlike old lead-acid packs, modern lithium units recharge in hours, hold a charge for months, and include safety circuitry that prevents sparks and reverse-polarity damage, making them genuinely foolproof for most drivers.

Buying tips

  • Size the jump starter to the biggest engine you own, including future vehicles, so you are never caught short on a diesel or V8.
  • Recharge the pack every 3 to 6 months, even when idle, since lithium cells slowly self-discharge and lose peak output.
  • Check clamp and cable quality: thick, well-insulated clamps with solid spring tension make safer, more reliable connections.
  • Keep the unit in the cabin rather than the trunk during winter, since extreme cold reduces battery chemistry and can prevent a successful jump.

Frequently asked questions

How many amps do I need to jump start a car?

A typical 4-cylinder gas engine starts reliably with a 1000-amp peak jump starter, while 6-cylinder engines benefit from 1000 to 1500 amps. For V8s, larger SUVs, and diesel trucks, look for 2000 amps or more to ensure enough cranking power in cold conditions.

Can a jump starter damage my car?

A quality jump starter with reverse-polarity and spark-proof protection will not damage your car, even if you connect the clamps incorrectly. Cheap or unprotected units without these safety circuits can send voltage spikes that harm your battery, alternator, or sensitive electronics.

How long does a jump starter hold charge?

Most modern lithium jump starters retain a usable charge for 3 to 6 months when stored at room temperature. To guarantee readiness, top up the battery every few months and avoid leaving it in extreme heat or cold.

What is the best jump starter for diesel trucks?

Diesel engines need high compression, so choose a 2000-amp or higher unit like the NOCO GB70, AVAPOW A07, or TOPDON JS2000. These packs deliver the sustained cranking power diesels demand, especially in cold weather when the engine turns over hardest.

Are cheap jump starters safe?

Budget jump starters can be safe if they include reverse-polarity protection, spark-proof clamps, and over-current safeguards. Avoid unbranded units that omit these circuits, since a failed protection board can short your battery or damage your vehicle electronics.
