Which SUV has the most spacious third row?

Est. cost: $40000–$90000 USD

Quick answer

The Chevrolet Suburban, GMC Yukon XL, and Ford Expedition Max lead the segment with roughly 36-37 inches of third-row legroom—enough for average adults to sit comfortably on long trips. The Cadillac Escalade ESV matches those dimensions in a more luxurious package, while midsize options like the Kia Telluride and Volvo XC90 trail closer to 31 inches.

Third-row space has become a decisive factor for families shopping large SUVs, because a cramped way-back defeats the purpose of buying a three-row vehicle. When we talk about a "spacious" third row, we are really weighing three measurements together: third-row legroom (the distance from the seat cushion to the front of the backrest ahead), third-row headroom (how much vertical clearance adults get), and the cargo volume that remains when all seats are folded flat. A high legroom number is the single best proxy for adult comfort, but headroom and seat cushion length matter almost as much for avoiding the knees-up "kiddie" posture.

The trade-off is real: the SUVs with the roomiest third rows are body-on-frame full-size models that stretch over 220 inches long, weigh nearly 6,000 pounds, and return fuel economy in the mid-teens. They are harder to park and more expensive to run than a unibody midsize crossover, so buyers should weigh genuine third-row usability against the day-to-day penalties of driving a true full-size truck-based SUV. For buyers who truly need to carry six adults regularly, that penalty is usually worth it.

Frequently asked questions

Which 3-row SUV has the most cargo?

The Chevrolet Suburban and GMC Yukon XL lead the segment with up to 144.7 cubic feet of cargo volume behind the front seats. Among midsize crossovers, the Kia Telluride and Honda Pilot top out around 87 cubic feet, roughly 40% less than the full-size body-on-frame leaders.

What is the most spacious midsize 3-row SUV?

The Kia Telluride (and its Hyundai Palisade twin) is widely regarded as the most spacious midsize three-row SUV, offering 31.4 inches of third-row legroom and 87 cubic feet of max cargo. The Toyota Grand Highlander and Honda Pilot are close rivals in overall interior volume.

Can adults fit in the third row of an SUV?

Yes, in full-size SUVs like the Suburban, Yukon XL, and Expedition Max, adults up to about six feet tall can sit comfortably in the third row thanks to roughly 36-37 inches of legroom. In most midsize crossovers, the third row is realistically best suited for children, teenagers, or short adult trips.

Which SUV has the most comfortable third row?

Comfort depends on legroom, headroom, and cushion support together. The Cadillac Escalade ESV is often cited as the most comfortable thanks to its air suspension, thick padding, and adult-sized 36.7-inch legroom, while the Lincoln Navigator L and Mercedes-Benz GLS also rank highly for plush, well-padded third-row seating.

Is a full-size SUV worth it just for the third row?

It depends on how often you carry six or more adults. If that is a weekly occurrence, the superior third-row comfort and cargo of a Suburban, Yukon XL, or Expedition Max easily justify the higher fuel and parking costs. If the third row is only for occasional kid-hauling, a midsize crossover like the Telluride is far more practical day to day.
