What are the best winter tires for an SUV?

Est. cost: $100–$300 USD

Quick answer

The Michelin CrossClimate 2 SUV is the best all-round pick, carrying the three-peak mountain snowflake (3PMSF) symbol for genuine winter grip while remaining strong in summer. For dedicated deep-snow and ice performance, the Bridgestone Blizzak DM-V2 is the standout studless choice for SUVs. Always look for the 3PMSF mark rather than a simple M+S rating, as only the former certifies real severe-snow capability.

SUVs carry extra weight and a higher center of gravity, so a proper winter tire is essential to translate all that mass into safe, controlled grip on snow and ice. The two ratings you will see most are M+S (mud and snow), which only describes the tread pattern, and the three-peak mountain snowflake (3PMSF), which certifies the tire passed an actual acceleration test on medium-packed snow. For true winter capability you want the 3PMSF symbol. Within winter tires you also choose between studless compounds (soft, quiet, excellent on ice, legal everywhere) and studded tires (metal pins for extreme ice, but restricted or banned on many roads). For most SUV drivers a modern studless 3PMSF tire offers the best blend of safety, comfort, and year-round legality.

How we chose

  • Winter grip and snow performance verified by the 3PMSF certification and independent deep-snow traction testing.
  • Ice braking distance, since stopping on glare ice is the most safety-critical metric for a heavy SUV.
  • Treadwear and warranty, including expected mileage and manufacturer coverage for even-wear replacement.
  • Wet traction and hydroplaning resistance to keep SUVs stable on slushy and rainy winter roads.
  • Price per tire balanced against performance, recognizing that premium studless compounds cost more but last longer.

Buying tips

  • Verify the exact tire size, load index, and speed rating printed on your SUV door jamb placard before ordering.
  • Always mount a matching set of four winter tires; mixing tread types on an AWD SUV can damage the drivetrain and unbalance stability control.
  • Consider a pre-mounted wheel-and-tire package so you can swap the set yourself each season and protect your summer alloys.
  • Switch to winter tires when daily temperatures stay consistently below 7 C / 45 F, and remove them in spring to preserve the soft compound.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need winter tires if my SUV has AWD?

Yes. All-wheel drive helps you accelerate, but it does nothing for braking or cornering on snow and ice. Winter tires provide the grip needed to stop and turn safely, which is where AWD falls short. AWD plus winter tires is the safest combination.

Are all-season tires enough for an SUV in winter?

For light cold weather, maybe, but most all-season tires lack the 3PMSF rating and stiffen below 7 C. In real snow and ice they brake and corner far worse than a dedicated winter tire. If you see regular snow or sustained sub-freezing temps, dedicated winters are worth it.

Studded or studless winter tires?

Studded tires give maximum grip on bare ice but are noisy, wear roads, and are restricted or banned in many regions. Modern studless tires with silica-rich compounds now close most of the ice-performance gap while staying quiet and legal everywhere, so studless is the better choice for most SUV owners.

How long do winter tires last?

Winter tires typically last three to six seasons, or roughly 30,000 to 50,000 km depending on driving style and mileage. Replace them when tread depth drops below 4 mm, since below that snow grip drops sharply even if the tire is not legally worn out.

What is the best budget winter tire for an SUV?

The Goodyear Ultra Grip Ice SUV offers strong 3PMSF-rated snow and ice performance at a lower price than premium options. Other value picks include the General Altimax Arctic and Kumho WinterCraft, all of which deliver solid winter grip for under about 150 USD per tire.
